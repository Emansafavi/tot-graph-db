# Summary
This paper studies gaze-supported touch interaction on handheld devices and frames touch as a confirming gesture in target acquisition. It seems relevant mainly as an interaction-technique reference and less as a central conceptual contribution to technologies of touch. Your note suggests it may be more inspirational in method and testing logic than in theoretical content.

# Key Concepts
- gaze-supported touch
- target acquisition
- touch as confirmation
- interaction technique

# Notes
A key idea here is that gaze suggests and touch confirms.

The problem context seems to involve scenarios where the user is not at a conventional desktop setup, such as being on a couch or in front of a large display wall.

At the moment this looks peripheral to your main conceptual direction, but it may still be useful as a comparison case for how touch is operationalized in HCI experiments.

# Quotes
