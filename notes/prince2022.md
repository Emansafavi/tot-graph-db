# Summary
This paper frames digital touch as a communicative, relational, social, and cultural practice rather than merely a sensory or physiological phenomenon. It expands the discourse of touch beyond device-centric or implementation-focused views. It is useful as a conceptual anchor for understanding technologies of touch as a broader field.

# Key Concepts
- digital touch
- communicative practice
- relational touch
- affective touch
- socio-cultural touch

# Notes
This functions almost like a manifesto for expanded theorization of touch.

It is especially useful for framing touch as a practice rather than an input modality.

The paper helps articulate what is often missing in technical domains where touch is reduced to sensing, control, or novelty.

This should probably become one of the central nodes in the graph.

# Quotes
