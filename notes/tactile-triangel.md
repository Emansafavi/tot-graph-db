# Summary
This paper proposes the tactile triangle as a three-level framework for understanding touch through physical properties, perceptual experience, and communication. It treats touch as something measurable, perceivable, and communicable rather than only as a sensory event. It is useful as a transferable model for analysing touch across domains beyond textiles.

# Key Concepts
- tactile triangle
- physical properties
- perceptual space
- communication of tactile experience
- touch as multi-layered phenomenon

# Notes
The research is framed around a triangle between designers and experts, consumers as non-experts, and mechanized objectives.

This is one of the most useful conceptual papers in the set because it gives a structure for talking about touch at multiple levels.

The model can be mapped onto musical interface design:
Physical layer = hardware and sensing infrastructure
Perceptual layer = performer’s tactile experience
Communication layer = vocabulary of expressive touch

This is especially strong because it exposes a gap in fields where touch is discussed mostly in terms of sensing and implementation, while perceptual and communicative layers remain underdeveloped.
