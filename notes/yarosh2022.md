# Summary
This paper studies mediated social touch in remote communication and compares visual, haptic, and multimodal symbolic gestures. It argues that touch can operate as a symbolic communication channel whose meaning depends on relationship and emotional context. It is especially interesting because physical haptics are not always necessary for meaningful mediated touch.

# Key Concepts
- mediated social touch
- symbolic communication
- social presence
- social dissonance
- multimodal gestures

# Notes
This paper is important because it partially decouples touch from physical haptics.

A projector-camera system without haptics can perform no worse than a haptic one on several measures, which is conceptually very useful.

It suggests that symbolic touch in digital contexts can sometimes stand in for physical touch.

This is a good example of a paper that stays methodologically rigorous while still addressing what touch means, not only how it is technically implemented.
